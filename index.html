In this course, I helped students with more hands-on engineering ____ such as: CAD & 3D printing, laser-cutting, advanced Arduino & circuits, writing technical reports, as well as general advising in regards to their projects and how to go about implementing their ideas.

More About Me

Throughout school I've always enjoyed math and science, but working at my family's salvage yard and dirt bike track is where I gained my love and appreciation for engineering. I originally enrolled at CU Boulder with the intention of majoring in Aerospace Engineering. When I made it into the program, I realized that I had lost a part of what drew me to engineering in the first place: creativity.

My dream has always been to create, and the appeal for the prospect of engineering was exactly that: creating using math and science. While I found the curriculum to be fascinating, I felt that the projects lacked that creative thinking that I longed for. That's when I heard about the Atlas Institute at CU and fell in love instantly. Project-based curriculum built around creative freedom through engineering.

Aside from engineering, my other passions include motocross, writing, and music.

Future

As for the future, I plan on graduating from school with my Bachelor's in May of 2025, and then going on to graduate school to get my Master's Degree.

Career-wise, I would be happy as long as I get to do a job that's hands-on and mentally stimulating; anything that centers around creative problem-solving.
